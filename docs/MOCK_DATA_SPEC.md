# docs/MOCK_DATA_SPEC.md — Fixtures and Seeding Rules

The MVP uses mock adapters that return data shaped exactly like the real adapters will. Fixtures live in `packages/fixtures/` as JSON. Seed scripts in `apps/api/seeds/` generate rows from these fixtures.

## §goals

The seeded demo should look like a real working terminal at 3pm on a Friday in winter, with believable market structure, recent storage data, and a credible signal pattern. It should not look obviously synthetic. The tells that make synthetic data look fake are:
- prices that move in too-smooth lines
- volume that doesn't correlate with volatility
- volatility that doesn't cluster
- forecasts that are always confident
- COT data that doesn't change week-to-week
- news events that all have the same impact score

The fixtures and seed scripts are deliberate about avoiding these.

## §price_bars

Generated, not stored verbatim. Generator: `apps/api/seeds/price_generator.py`.

- Front-month NG, 730 trading days back to today.
- Geometric Brownian motion with regime-switching volatility:
  - `compressed` regime: σ = 0.18, mean drift 0
  - `normal` regime:     σ = 0.32, mean drift small
  - `elevated` regime:   σ = 0.55, slight upward drift
  - `crisis` regime:     σ = 0.90, no drift, fat tails
- Regime transitions follow a 4-state Markov chain calibrated so the path spends roughly: 25% / 50% / 20% / 5% of time in each regime.
- Open ≈ previous close + small overnight gap. High/low have realistic relationship to o/c (no crosses).
- Volume: `base * (1 + 2*|return|)` with a weekly seasonality (Mondays + Thursdays heavier).
- Seasonality overlay: a soft cosine that gently raises winter prices and softens summer prices, scaled so it doesn't dominate the random walk.
- 1-minute bars for the most recent 14 trading days, generated by the same model at higher resolution and aggregated for consistency.

Front-month is a single fictional contract `NGF26` with expiry `2026-01-28`. The generator also produces a 12-month curve at each daily close (next 12 monthly expiries) for `futures_curve_snapshots`, with a normal contango shape that occasionally flips to slight backwardation during synthetic "cold weather" stretches.

## §eia_storage_reports

Generated, weekly Thursdays at 14:30 ET for 2 years. Generator: `apps/api/seeds/storage_generator.py`.

- Annual storage cycle: ~1500 Bcf trough in late March, ~3700 Bcf peak in early November.
- Weekly net change follows the seasonal pattern + noise.
- `consensus_estimate` differs from actual by N(0, 8) Bcf — believable analyst miss.
- `surprise_bcf = net_change_bcf - consensus_estimate`.
- 5-year average / max / min derived from a synthetic 5-year series.
- Regional splits (East / Midwest / Mountain / Pacific / South Central) sum to total.

## §cot_reports

Generated, weekly Tuesdays for 2 years (released the following Friday). Generator: `apps/api/seeds/cot_generator.py`.

- **Per-symbol (Phase 17).** `generate(symbol)` emits 100 weekly reports for each
  of NG/CL/HO/RB/GC/SI, keyed by the market's CFTC code. `demo.py` step 4 loops
  the `COT_PARAMS` table. Every absolute magnitude (baseline, biases, noise std,
  OI floor) scales by `oi_baseline / 1.4M`, and each symbol draws from its own
  seeded RNG. NG keeps `seed=42` and a 1.4M baseline, so its output is unchanged.
- Open-interest baselines (approx): NG 1.4M, CL 1.8M, HO 300k, RB 300k, GC 500k,
  SI 150k; slow ±10% drift.
- Managed-money net oscillates within a per-symbol band (NG −150k…+250k; bands
  scale down for smaller markets), momentum-following (lags price by ~2 weeks).
- Producer net position is the inverse of managed-money net (commercials hedge against speculator positioning), with smoothing.
- Swap dealer position relatively stable.
- Categories sum correctly (long_total + short_total + nonreportable = 2 × open_interest).

## §weather_observations / weather_forecasts

Generated, daily for 6 regions for 60 days back + 14-day forward forecast. Generator: `apps/api/seeds/weather_generator.py`.

Regions: `northeast`, `midwest`, `mountain`, `pacific`, `south_central`, `southeast`.

- Seasonal mean temperature curve per region (climatological normals).
- Daily anomaly drawn from N(0, 6°F) with AR(1) persistence (today's anomaly correlates with yesterday's).
- Occasional "weather event" stretches: a 5-10 day deviation of -10°F to -15°F (cold snap) or +10°F to +15°F (heat wave), randomly inserted ~3 times per year.
- HDD = max(0, 65 - temp_f) per day, summed weekly elsewhere.
- CDD = max(0, temp_f - 65) per day.
- Forecast = observation + N(0, σ_horizon) where σ grows with horizon: 1.5°F at 1d, 6°F at 14d.

## §news_events

Curated fixtures, not generated. 50 events spanning 2 years in `packages/fixtures/news_events.json`.

Each event: `published_at`, `source`, `headline`, `body`, `category`, `sentiment`, `impact_score`, `affected_regions`, `entities`. Categories are evenly distributed across `production`, `demand`, `weather`, `policy`, `geopolitical`, `infrastructure`.

Headlines are written to be plausibly real but obviously synthetic on close read (no real company names, no real officials by name). They reference the synthetic price and storage events at consistent times so the timeline tells a coherent story.

## §model_forecasts

Not pre-seeded. Forecasts are generated on demand by the model registry (`services/model_registry.py`) when an endpoint requests them, then persisted. The seed step does run the registry once to populate "latest" forecasts so the dashboard isn't empty on first load.

## §scenario_runs

5 sample runs are pre-created so the Scenario Lab has history immediately:
1. Cold Snap Northeast 10 days
2. LNG export disruption (Sabine Pass-style outage) 14 days
3. Production freeze-off in Appalachia
4. Hurricane disruption Gulf Coast
5. Geopolitical shock (synthetic European demand spike)

Each is a complete `scenarios/run` response stored verbatim.

## §journal_and_paper_trades

3 sample journal entries, of which one has a closed paper trade with positive PnL and one has a closed paper trade with negative PnL. The third is open.

These exist mainly so the Decision Journal and Paper Trading screens have content on first load and so the LLM journal-review feature can be demonstrated.

## §fixture_files

```
packages/fixtures/
  instruments.json          # 1 row, NG
  contracts.json            # 12 rows
  news_events.json          # 50 curated events
  scenario_templates.json   # 6 preset shock packages
  scenario_runs.seed.json   # 5 historical runs
  journals.seed.json        # 3 entries
  paper_trades.seed.json    # 3 trades
  alerts.seed.json          # 5 sample alerts
```

Generated tables (`price_bars`, `eia_storage_reports`, `cot_reports`, `weather_*`) do not have JSON fixtures; they are produced by the generators above.

## §randomness_and_determinism

Seed generator uses `random.Random(seed=42)` and `numpy.random.default_rng(seed=42)` everywhere so the demo is fully reproducible. Re-running `/seed-natgas` produces byte-identical fixtures unless the generator code itself changed.

## §quality_check

The seed step concludes by running `apps/api/seeds/validate.py`, which asserts:
- bar OHLC invariants (`o`, `c` between `l` and `h`; `h >= l`)
- COT category sums match open interest
- EIA region splits sum to total
- weather forecast horizons are present for the next 14 days for every region
- at least one bullish, one bearish, and one neutral forecast exists in `model_forecasts`
- no string in any fixture matches a phrase from `docs/AI_BEHAVIOR.md §forbidden_phrases`

If any assertion fails, the seed exits non-zero and the demo will not start.
